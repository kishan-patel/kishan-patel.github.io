### Chapter 1 - Financial Statements and Business Decisions
<!-- Q7 -->
<font color="red">
<pre>
Q7- What are the purposes of:  
(a) the statement of earnings,  
(b) the statement of financial position,   
(c) the statement of cash flows,  
(d) the statement of changes in equity?  
</pre>
</font>
<font color="blue">
<pre>
<b>a- Statement of earnings</b>: Provides information about the revenue, expenses, and net earnings for a specified period of time.
<b>b- Statement of financial position</b>: Provides information about the assets, liabilities, and shareholders equity at a given date.
<b>c- Statement of cash flows</b>: Provides information about the inflow of cash, outflow of cash, and the net increase or decrease in cashfor for a specified period.
<b>d- Statement of changes in equity</b>: Provides information on how the net earnings, distribution of net earnings, and other changes to shareholders' equity change the financial position of a company for a specified period of time.
</pre>
</font>

<!-- Q10 -->
<font color="red">
<pre>	
Q10- Briefly define net earnings and net loss.
</pre>
</font>
<font color="blue">
<pre>
If revenue - cost > 0, we have net earnings

If revenue - cost < 0, we have a net loss
</pre>
</font>

<!-- Q11 -->
<font color="red">
<pre>
Q11- Explain the accounting equation for the statement of earnings. Define the three major items reported on the statement of earnings.
</pre>
</font>
<font color="blue">
<pre>
<b>Equation</b>: Revenue - Expenses = Net Earnings/Net Loss

<b>Revenue</b>: Monetary value of goods and services sold, regardless of when cash is collected from the customer.

<b>Expenses</b>: Monetary value of resources that were used to earn the revenue during the period, regardless of when cash is paid

<b>Net Earnings</b>: The excess of revenues over expenses.

<b>Net Loss</b>: The excess of expenses over revenues.
</pre>
</font>

<!-- Q12 -->
<font color="red">
<pre>
Q12- Explain the accounting equation for the statement of financial position. Define the three major components reported on the statement of financial position.
</pre>
</font>
<font color="blue">
<pre>
<b>Equation</b>: Assets = Liabilities + Shareholders' Equity

<b>Assets</b>: Economic resources controlled by an entity as a result of past business events that have potential to contribute to cash to the entity in the future.

<b>Liabilities</b>: Entity's obligations that result from past business events. They arise primarily from purchasing goods or services on credit and borrowing cash to finance the business.

<b>Shareholders' Equity</b>: Represents the amount of financing provided by the owners of the business and reinvested earnings.
</pre>
</font>

<!-- Q13 -->
<font color="red">
<pre>
Q13- Explain the accounting equation for the statement of cash flows. Explain the three major components reported on the statement.
</pre>
</font>
<font color="blue">
<pre>
<b>Equation</b>: Cashflow from operating activities +/- Cashflow from investing activities +/- Cashflow from financing activities = Change in cash flow during the period

<b>Cashflow from operating activities</b>: Amount of money a company brings in from ongoing, regular business activities, such as manufacturing and selling of goods or providing a service.

<b>Cashflow from investing activities</b>: Includes cash flows related to the acquisition or sale of the company's assets (e.g. plant and equipment)

<b>Cashflow from financing activities</b>: Includes cash flows related to the financing of the business (e.g. issuance of shares)
</pre>
</font>

<!-- Q14 -->
<font color="red">
<pre>
Q14- Explain the accounting equation for retained earnings. Explain the major items that affect the ending balance of retained earnings.
</pre>
</font>
<font color="blue">
<pre>
<b>Equation</b>: Beginning retained earnings + Net earnings - Dividends paid = Ending retained earnings

<b>Beginning retained earnings</b>: Accumulated net earnings of the company through the years which have been re-invested in the company

<b>Net earnings</b>: Profit (or loss) for the current year

<b>Dividends</b>: Amount paid to shareholders
</pre>
</font>

<!-- Q17 -->
<font color="red">
<pre>
Q17- Briefly explain the responsibilty of company management, the board of directors, and the independent auditors in the internal control and financial reporting process.
</pre>
</font>
<font color="blue">
<pre>
<b>Company management</b>: Must sign certification to show that they've supervised the design, implementation, and evaluation of the financial accounting reporting process.

<b>Board of directors</b>: Holds the officers of the company accountable to the shareholders.

<b>Independent auditors</b>: Provide advice to the company on how to best comply with regualations on financial reporting.
</pre>
</font>

</pre>

<font color="blue">
<pre>
a. Asset: Economic resource controlled by the business as a result of a past business event from which future econmic benefits may be obtained.

b. Current asset: Asset that will be used or turned into cash within one year. Inventory is always considered a current asset regardless of how long it takes to produce and sell the inventory.

d. Current liability: Liability that will be paid with assets or services within the coming year.

e. Contributed capital: Cash (or assets) provided to the business by owners.
