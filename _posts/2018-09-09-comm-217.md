## Chapter Summaries

### Chapter 1 - Financial Statements and Business Decisions
<b>Shareholder</b>:  
Investors who buy small percentages of large corporations. They hope to make money in two ways:  
1- They expect to receive a portion of what the company earns in the form of cash payments called dividends  
2- They hope to eventually share their share of the company at a higher price than they paid  

<b>Creditors</b>:  
Lend money to a company for a specific length of time. They gain by charging interest on the money the lend.  

<b>The Business Operations</b>:  
1- Purchase products and access to services demanded by the customer  
2- Market the product and services available for sale  
3- Sell products and services to customers  
4- Collect cash from customer and pay suppliers  

<b>Internal Decision Makers</b>:  
Mangers are internal decision makers who need information about the company's business activities to control and improve the operating, investing, and financing activities of the firm.

<b>External Decision Makers</b>:  
Shareholder and creditors are external decision makers who need information about the business activities to assess whether the company will be able to pay back its debts with interest and pay dividends.

<b>Accounting System</b>:  
Collects and processess useful financial information about an organization and reports that information to decision makers.  

<b>Examples of Business Activities</b>:  
1- Financing Activities: borrowing or paying back money to lenders and receiving additional funds from shareholders, or paying them dividends  
2- Investing Activities: buying or selling items, such as retail stores, buildings, and equipment used to store and then distribute goods and services to customers  
3- Operating Activities: the day-to-day process of obtaining products and services from suppliers, storing and then delivering them to customers, collecting cash from customers, and paying suppliers  

<b>Four Basic Financial Statements</b>:  
1- Statement of Financial Position: Purpose is to report the financial position (amount of assets, liabilities, and shareholders' equity) of an accounting entity at a particular point in time  
2- Statement of Earnings  
3- Statement of Changes in Equity  
4- Statement of Cash Flows  


<b>Statement of Financial Position Contents</b>:  
1- name of the accounting entity (e.g. Canadian Tire Corporation)  
2- title of the statment  (e.g. Statement of Financial Position)  
3- specific date of the statement (e.g. At Juanuary 2, 2016)  
4- unit of measure (e.g. in millions of Canadian Dollars)
